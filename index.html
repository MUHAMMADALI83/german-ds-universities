How to Apply

Germany uses a two-semester system. Winter Semester (October) is the main intake — the vast majority of Data Science bachelor programs open here. Summer Semester (April) has limited availability for bachelor programs. Always check the specific program's official page.
01
Choose Your Program
Decide on English-taught vs German-taught. Check if the university accepts non-EU students for bachelor's programs and which language certificate is required.
02
Get Language Certified
German track: DSH-2 or TestDaF 4×4. English track: IELTS 6.0–6.5 or TOEFL 80–90+. Some universities require both for bilingual programs.
03
Apply via Uni-Assist
Most German public universities require non-EU applicants to go through uni-assist.de for document assessment (VPD). Apply 6–8 weeks before the university deadline.
04
Open Blocked Account
Proof of financial resources: €11,208/year (~€934/month) in a German blocked account (Sperrkonto). Required for student visa application.
05
Apply to University
Submit your application via the university's online portal (e.g., TUMonline for TUM, ALMA for Tübingen) before the stated deadline — typically July 15 for winter.
06
Get Your Visa
After receiving your admission letter, apply for a student visa at the German Embassy. Allow 8–12 weeks. Some countries (India, China) need an APS certificate too.
